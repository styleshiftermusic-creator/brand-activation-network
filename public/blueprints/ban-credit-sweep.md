## METHOD 1: 24-Hour Hard Inquiry Removal (Phone Method)

Hard inquiries are logged every time a lender pulls your credit file. The problem: **many of them are unauthorized, unverifiable, or fraudulent** — and they are silently dragging your score down every day you leave them untouched.

Under the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**, any inquiry that cannot be verified as authorized by you **must be removed**. Bureaus have a legal obligation to investigate — and if they can't verify it, they delete it.

**Common unauthorized inquiry sources:**
- Car dealerships that ran multiple pulls without disclosure
- Stolen identity or data breach activity
- Inquiries tied to accounts you never opened
- "Soft pull" inquiries that showed up as hard pulls

This blueprint gives you the exact phone numbers, script, and process to challenge those inquiries directly through each bureau's **Fraud Department** — the division with the authority to act immediately.

## CHAPTER 4: Which Inquiries Qualify for Removal

### ✅ CAN Be Removed
- Inquiries **not tied to any open or closed account** in your file
- Inquiries where you have **no record of applying** with that lender
- Any inquiry that is **2+ years old** (bureaus are legally required to remove these immediately)
- Denied account inquiries older than 1 year

### ❌ CANNOT Be Removed
- Inquiries directly tied to an account you did open

**Example:**
- You opened a Chase Sapphire in May 2020 → The Chase inquiry from May 2020 **stays**
- You have an American Express inquiry but zero Amex accounts → That inquiry **can be removed**

> **Pro Tip:** Pull your full credit report before calling. Cross-reference every inquiry against your list of open and closed accounts. Only challenge inquiries with no matching account.

## CHAPTER 5: The Step-by-Step Process

**Before You Call:**
1. Pull your credit reports from all three bureaus
2. List every hard inquiry you want challenged (name of creditor + date)
3. Confirm each one has no matching account in your file

**On the Call:**
1. Navigate IVR to the Fraud Department
2. State you are disputing unauthorized hard inquiries under the FCRA
3. Read off your target inquiries one by one
4. If placed on hold — stay on the line. This is normal.
5. The rep will confirm they are "launching an investigation"

**After the Call:**
1. Note the time, date, and rep's name or ID number
2. Monitor your credit report within 24–48 hours
3. Most inquiries are removed by the **next business day**
4. Equifax may take up to **48 hours**
5. Legal investigation window can extend to **30 days** under FCRA — but most resolve faster

## METHOD 2: The 7-Day Credit Sweep (Data Breach Method)

This method targets **negative accounts, fraudulent items, and collections** using the legal framework of identity theft protection. It is slower than the phone method but works on a broader range of derogatory items.

**Best for:** Collections, charge-offs, fraudulent accounts, or derogatory marks that aren't responding to standard disputes.

---

### STEP 1: Pull All Credit Reports

Get copies from all three major bureaus:

- **Experian:** experian.com/free-credit-report
- **Equifax:** equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services
- **TransUnion:** transunion.com/free-credit-report
- Or use: **AnnualCreditReport.com** (official free source)

Review each report carefully. Flag every account, collection, or inquiry you believe is fraudulent, unauthorized, or the result of a data breach.

---

### STEP 2: Freeze All Credit Profiles

Place a **security freeze** (also called a credit freeze) on each bureau immediately. This prevents new fraudulent accounts from being opened in your name while your dispute is active.

| Bureau | Freeze Link |
|---|---|
| Experian | experian.com/freeze |
| Equifax | equifax.com/personal/credit-freeze |
| TransUnion | transunion.com/credit-freeze |

> A freeze does **not** affect your credit score and can be lifted at any time.

---

### STEP 3: File a Police Report Online

File an official identity theft report. You can do this at:

- **FTC (recommended):** IdentityTheft.gov — generates an official Identity Theft Report
- **Local Police:** File online through your city/county portal

**What to include in your report:**
- All fraudulent or unauthorized accounts
- Any inquiries you did not authorize
- Any closed accounts you didn't open

Save your report number — you may reference it in your letters.

---

### STEP 4: Send a 609 Letter to Each Bureau

A **609 Letter** is a formal dispute letter that invokes Section 609 of the Fair Credit Reporting Act, requiring bureaus to provide original source documentation for any item on your report. If they cannot produce it, they must remove it.

**Send via Certified Mail with Return Receipt** to each bureau. This creates a legal paper trail and starts the 30-day investigation clock.

**Bureau Mailing Addresses:**

```
Experian
P.O. Box 4500

Equifax Information Services

TransUnion Consumer Solutions

```

**What your 609 Letter should include:**
1. Your full legal name, address, SSN (last 4 only recommended), and date of birth
2. A list of every item you are disputing with account name and date
3. Statement citing your rights under FCRA Section 609
4. Copy of your FTC Identity Theft Report (optional but strengthens your case)
5. Your signature

---

### STEP 5: Wait for Response & Monitor

Credit bureaus have **30 days** to respond to your dispute by law.

**What typically happens:**
- Many items are removed within **7 days** if documentation cannot be verified
- Bureau will mail you results of investigation
- If item is removed — it's gone from your report
- If bureau "verifies" the item — you can escalate with an FTC or CFPB complaint

**Timeline:**
| Stage | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Certified mail delivery | 2–5 business days |
| Bureau acknowledges dispute | Within 5 days |
| Items removed (best case) | 7–14 days |
| Full legal investigation window | 30 days |
